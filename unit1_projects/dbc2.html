Guacamole: Beginner's Tutorial

It ain't easy being green

Ingredients Needed:

  • 2 ripe avocados
  • 1 tomato
  • 1 onion
  • 1 jalapeño pepper
  • 1 large lime
  • 1 tsp garlic salt
  • Cilantro (to taste)

Directions:

Guacamole can be a tough nut to crack. Pleae don't attempt this if:

  • You don't have ripe avocados. Unfortunately, this depends on the season and your location, so I'm not even going to try to instruct on this.
  • You don't like cilantro. Sorry to all those who, according to the New York Times, taste soap instead of this delicious herb.
  • You aren't willing to put in some elbow grease. More on this when we get to mixing.

With that in mind, follow these steps for heaven in a bowl.

  1. Using a serrated knife, slide each avocado in half. Remove the seed and slice avocado into large mixing bowl.
  2. Chop onion into small pieces, then add to mixing bowl. FYI: chewing mint gum counteracts your eyes' urge to cry. Try it!
  3. Chop tomato into small pieces, then add to mixing bowl.
  4. Slice jalapeño, then add to mixing bowl. Don't complain, just wait and be amazed.
  5. Slice lime in half, stab each half with a fork and juice into mixing bowl.
  6. Add garlic salt.
  7. To keep the cilantro as fresh as possible, we have saved it for the end. Chop up finely and add to taste, which doesn't mean to not add any.
  8. Use the bottom of a clean cup to mash and mix this beautiful concoction together.
